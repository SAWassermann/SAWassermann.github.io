Research

My primary research interest lies in computer networks, and mostly in network traffic measurements.
I am especially curious about discovering performance degradations on the server-to-customer internet paths of popular services (e.g. YouTube, Facebook, etc.). Being able to track and troubleshoot these issues is paramount for ISPs and will lead to a better quality of experience (QoE) for the users.
I have been interested in research since my 2nd year at university. This interest was strengthened by two Research Internships I performed in the last two years, in the specific field of Internet network measurements, and more precisely in active measurements for network performance analysis.

DisNETPerf

A distributed Internet paths performance analyzer developed in the context of my Research Internship @FTW Vienna. For more information about this work, please have a look at my papers or visit the Github repo of DisNETPerf.

libparistraceroute

During my internship @LiP6 in Paris, I implemented a generic ping tool based on libparistraceroute which can handle IPv4, IPv6 and TCP, UDP, ICMP probes. I also extended the library itself. More information can be found on the project's Google Code site.
